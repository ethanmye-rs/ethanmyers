Milling Machine

As I move into more intensive and detailed projects, I increasing need to fabricate custom parts. Normally, I get through this with a 3D printer. It’s allowed me to make objects far more complex than I can reliably make by hand. That being said, I’m beginning to run up against the limitations of plastic. Even with wondermaterials like PET and HIPS, there’s no easy way to build high temp / high precision / high accuracy / high stress / high wear parts. For bearing surfaces, 0.1mm just isn’t good enough.

Thus, I’ve been trawling Craigslist for a few months looking for a mill. While I would have preferred (immensely) a Bridgeport, there’s fairly few in Colorado to choose from, and I simply do not have the space. Maybe in a few years.

That being said, I needed a mill beefy enough to chew through steel and heavy enough so I can take more than a 10 thou cut in aluminum without destroying the machine. A desktop hobby mill, Sherline or x-carve just wasn’t going to work.

Finally, I found a JET mill/drill from 1979 for sale on Craigslist for $800, negotiated down $100, and took it home. The machine was cast in Taiwan, and had seen remarkably little use. It’s marked “JET 16”, and has a 12 speed belt drive.

Despite its age, I’m the third owner. Despite being clean on the surface, the ways were dirty with a mixture of oil and old, never cleaned packing grease. I cleaned it all up with some degreaser and a bit of brakekleen, and relubricated the ways with Vactra #2. I also lubricated the leadscrews with lithium grease, as well as the thrust bearings.
